## Post‑1995 Advances in MOST & Related ABL / Arctic Amplification Literature (Selected)

### Thematic Summary
- Extended stability functions (very stable / weak turbulence): Grachev et al. (2007, 2013), Sorbjan (2010), Gryanik et al. (2020) refine φ, ψ for SBL, emphasize z/L limits and Richardson critical behavior.
- Multi‑regime / composite scaling: Zilitinkevich & Esau (2005), Zilitinkevich et al. (2012) introduce additional external length scales (boundary layer depth h, N, Coriolis) → blending formulations.
- Intermittency & non‑stationarity: Mahrt (1998, 2014) documents regime splits (turbulent–weak turbulence), challenging steady MOST assumptions under weak shear / strong stability.
- Local vs surface scaling reassessment: Cheng & Brutsaert (2005) and Basu et al. (2006) test local scaling (Nieuwstadt) vs classical MOST; highlight collapse limits.
- Flux–variance / higher moments: Katul et al. (2006, 2011) extend similarity to scalar variance and dissipation; Salesky & Anderson (2011, 2018) refine convective mixed‑layer universal relationships.
- Roughness sublayer / canopy & urban extensions: Harman & Finnigan (2007), Li et al. (2018) integrate blending‑height concepts; urban parameter retrieval (Kent et al. 2017) influences effective z0, d in MOST application.
- Stable boundary layer criticality & Ri limits: Grachev et al. (2013) SHEBA analyses show continuous rather than sharp cutoff; motivates smooth φ forms (no abrupt turbulence collapse).
- Arctic SBL specifics: Esau & Sorokina (2010), Davy & Esau (2014) link inversion structure to amplification sensitivity; need curvature‑aware / depth‑aware similarity.
- Unified φ parameterizations with analytic integrals: Li, Katul & Bou‑Zeid (2012) supply closed forms spanning broader stability; Gryanik et al. (2020) propose modified stable φ with observational fit + model implementability.
- Machine‑learning / hybrid updates: Early ML (surface flux retrieval) complements MOST rather than replaces it; still reliant on physically constrained φ/ψ inputs.
- Arctic Amplification mechanisms: Shift from pure albedo feedback to multi‑factor (inversion trapping, turbulent mixing biases, cloud & moisture feedbacks) – Pithan & Mauritsen (2014), Boeke et al. (2021), Douville (2023).
- Richardson / Ri_g curvature use: Recent analytic work (your manuscript) targets grid dependence by embedding curvature into boundary conditions, extending classical flux–profile approach.
