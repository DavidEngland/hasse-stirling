The parametrized Hasse operator Hα,β,r\mathcal{H}_{\alpha,\beta,r}Hα,β,r​ is defined as:

Where Hm,nα,β,rH_{m,n}^{\alpha,\beta,r}Hm,nα,β,r​ are the Hasse coefficients.

    where CfC_fCf​ depends on bounds of the derivatives of fff.

  2. Exponential Decay:
    For many applications, the error decreases exponentially with MMM:
    Error≤K⋅e−λMMp\text{Error} \leq \frac{K \cdot e^{-\lambda M}}{M^p}Error≤MpK⋅e−λM​
    where KKK, λ\lambdaλ, and ppp are constants depending on α\alphaα, β\betaβ, rrr, and fff.

8. The Inverse Hasse Transform

Problem Statement

Given the transformation:
